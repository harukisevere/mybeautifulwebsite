hiragana is a syllabic alphabet that developed from сhinese cursive writing. hiragana symbols do not carry their own meaning and are used for recording the language by sound. оn average, they are used to write the "tails" (suffixes) of japanese words, but it can also be written exclusively with it: the less educated the intended readers of the text, the less kanji and more hiragana there are. it has a soft, smooth form, which is why in some texts it can even be used to emphasize female speech. unlike kanji, hiragana characters are limited to a small number of 48, and their diacritic variations: for example, か ka is read as が ga when the stressed diacritic ゛— handakuten — is added to the character.

私の黒い傘は美しい — [watashi no kurou kasa wa utsukushī] — "my black umbrella is beautiful". it can be written with hiragana only: わたくし の くろい かさ は うつくしい, but it's shorter, more readable and more beautiful to write it with kanji.
